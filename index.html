LONG GUNS

Rifles are permitted at this time and to be shot bench-rested only. Must demonstrate control of weapon and shoot into center mass only. Brass case and lead core ammo only. No steel core or armor piercing rounds. Ammo will be inspected upon arrival to the range. Magazines must be empty and ammo must be accessible for inspection. 

MILITARY DISCOUNT

All “ACTIVE” Military, Law Enforcement & First Responders ($17 unlimited time).

FIRST TIME SHOOTERS

If you have never shot a gun before; it is recommended that you schedule a One on One session for an hour with our Master instructors to become more comfortable with the range and firearm usage. You can start with a small weapon and work to a larger one as you desire. 

SAFETY

In the middle of the range there is a full-time range safety officer that will ensure that everyone is following the safety protocols of the range. There is a 0% tolerance policy to anyone not following proper safety rules. Safety officer is also available should you have a problems while on the range.

Range Safety Rules: 

•ALWAYS TREAT A FIREARM AS IF IT WERE LOADED

•KEEP THE GUN POINTED IN THE SAFEST DIRECTION

•FINGER OFF THE TRIGGER UNTIL YOU’RE READY TO SHOOT

•KNOW YOUR TARGET AND WHAT IS BEYOND 

Lowcountry Guns and range and staff reserve the right to check firearms and ammo. Anything found unsafe will not be permitted on the range.

FIREARMS MUST STAY IN A CASE UNTIL SET UP AT THE FIRING LINE.

•Alcoholic beverages and mind altering substances are prohibited.

•Rules are in English and must be understood, failure to comply may result in expulsion from premises.

•Do not leave or enter the building with a loaded firearm.

•1 shooter on the firing line at a time.

Lowcountry Guns and Range reserves the right to refuse attendance to anyone.

•Proper eye and ear protection must be worn at all times while in range.

•Only use correct ammunition for the gun you’re shooting.

•If a “cease fire” is called stop shooting immediately. Stand clear of your firearm and remain away from the firing line.

•Shooters must be 21 or older to rent or buy handguns or handgun ammunition. 18 years old for shotguns and rifles.

•Range officers must be obeyed promptly.

•No open toed shoes or tank tops. No squib load, tracer rounds, explosive rounds or armor piercing rounds.

•It is every member’s responsibility to read and understand all the posted safety rules and regulations as they pertain to this shooting venue. 

HEARING & EYE

Eye & Ear protection is to be worn at all times while on the range. Eye and ear protection is complimentary.

LOADING FIREARMS

Magazines/firearms are not to be loaded until you are at your firing bay and are ready to fire. Firearms must be open and on safe until you are ready to fire. NO EXCEPTIONS.

Transfers

People that send us transfers without notifying us will be subject to a $25 inconvenience fee.

FAQ

Common questions

What do I have to wear?

Close-toed shoes and crew neck shirts are required to go into the range. No flip-flops, sleeveless or lowcut tops. 

Can my children shoot at your range?

Yes. All shooters under the age of 18 must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian 21 or older. Caliber’s must be approved by the Range Officer.

How much does it cost to use the range?

$22.00 per person / per visit. There is no time limit!! Brass case and lead core only. No steel core, tracer  or armor piercing rounds. We will check your ammo & firearm before you go into the range. (We also offer annual memberships for those individuals and families who are frequent shooters.)

Can I shoot an AR-15 at your range?

Yes, must be able to control recoil. No rapid fire. Rifle caliber firearms must be bench rested and on target at all times. 

Can I shoot a Shotgun?

Yes, we only allow slugs and will check ammo before admission in the range. You must be able to control the firearm.

Is there firearms training available?

Yes, we do offer training for handguns and rifles as well as one on one training with a master instructor.
